Why this matters: the economics of expansion vs. acquisition

The path to 2x blended ROAS isn't faster acquisition or better landing pages alone — it's giving the customers Augment already has a second, third, and fourth thing to buy.

The VSL flow (5-10 min total runtime — this is the main sales engine)

The VSL is doing the actual selling on this page. Everything below it (offer card, value stack, testimonials, guarantee section) is reinforcement and proof. A 3-4 minute teaser doesn't do that job — the VSL needs full persuasion runtime to walk the buyer through the problem, the solution, the mechanism, and enough proof to make the decision feel safe. Target: 7-9 minutes. Long enough to do real selling work, short enough that completion rates stay high on mobile.

Seven beats, each doing a specific job:

  1. Hook (0:00-0:45). Open on the host direct to camera with a pattern-interrupt that names the buyer's actual frustration: "You've been told you need an MBA to run a real company. You've also probably realized the people teaching MBAs have never actually run one. There's a different way to learn this — and it's not what you think." Tee up the contrast. Don't reveal the solution yet — that's beat 4. The goal: make the buyer say "yes, that's the problem I have" in the first 45 seconds.

The traditional MBA was designed for corporate managers, not for people building from zero. The textbooks are five years out of date by the time you read them. The professors are smart academics — but they've never raised a round, launched a product, fired an underperformer, picked a co-founder, or scaled past a million in revenue. They studied business from the outside. You need to learn it from people who've done it." This is the bigger problem statement — founder isolation, generic education, the gap between theory and the actual hard decisions an operator makes weekly. The goal: make the cost of the WRONG education feel real.

Future-pace the next 12 months without Augment: continuing to learn from podcasts and Twitter threads, making the same expensive mistakes other operators already solved, watching peers who got the right mentorship pull ahead. "The cost isn't the $1,715 you'd spend on Augment. It's the year you'll spend figuring out things our 10,000+ students learned in 90 days." Why the ladder is structurally elegant — the self-feeding flywheel

Every live Accelerator (Tier 2) produces a recording. That recording becomes Tier 1 stock the following month. The library compounds:
